About CEMS

We are a skill development initiative undertaken by the Ministry of Ports, Shipping & Waterways, along with Siemens Industry Software India Pvt. Ltd. and the Indian Register of Shipping (IRCLASS), in alignment with the Prime Minister's "Skill India" initiative.

The project focuses on building competencies for Industry 4.0-driven Logistics and Supply Chain Management, offering training in advanced digital technologies, automation tools, and smart logistics solutions used in the global logistics and manufacturing sectors.

CEMS serves as a comprehensive training hub dedicated to imparting high-end, industry-relevant skills across the logistics and supply chain spectrum — from warehouse management, inventory optimization, transportation planning, and port logistics, to digital supply chain transformation. Our goal is to enable learners and professionals to harness Digital Logistics and Supply Chain 4.0 concepts, enhancing efficiency, visibility, and cost optimization across value chains.

Strategically located at Mumbai and Visakhapatnam, CEMS houses 18 state-of-the-art laboratories equipped to simulate real-world logistics operations, digital twin environments, and smart manufacturing ecosystems — bridging the gap between traditional operations and next-generation digital logistics.

Our Mission

To promote the advancement and implementation of logistics and supply chain skills aligned with advanced digital and automation technologies, through strong industry-academia collaboration, research, and experiential learning — empowering India's workforce to meet the evolving demands of global trade and logistics.

Vision

To establish CEMS as an International Centre of Excellence in Logistics and Supply Chain 4.0 for South Asia and South East Asia, attracting learners from neighboring countries.

We aim to serve as a knowledge and innovation hub, fostering leadership in integrated logistics, smart warehousing, port management, and digital supply chain ecosystems, driving maximum efficiency and sustainability in end-to-end supply chain operations.

Transportation Consolidator

Overview
A transportation or freight consolidator is a company that combines multiple smaller shipments from different shippers into a single, larger shipment, such as a full container load. This process reduces shipping costs for all parties involved by enabling them to access bulk shipping rates and optimizes transportation efficiency. Consolidators handle the coordination of these smaller shipments at a central hub before the consolidated load is transported.

Port Logistics

Overview
Port logistics is the management of all activities that involve the movement, handling, and storage of goods as they pass through a port, connecting different modes of transportation. This includes everything from unloading cargo from ships and storing it in yards or warehouses to its final delivery by land, and it is a crucial part of the global supply chain. Efficient port logistics reduces costs, minimizes delays, and is vital for international trade.

Consignment Agent

Overview
A consignment agent is a third-party intermediary who sells goods on behalf of an owner (the consignor) for a commission or fee. The agent takes possession of the goods, markets them, and handles the logistics, but the consignor retains ownership until the goods are sold. This model is common in industries like retail, art, and electronics, and allows the consignor to expand their market reach without managing direct sales, according to IndiaMART.
